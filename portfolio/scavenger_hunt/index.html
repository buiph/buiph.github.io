RAM's Scavenger Hunt

A demo of a scavenger hunt game that uses QR codes as a core mechanic. Developed for the Royal Alberta Museum as part of my capstone project.

  • Code samples available upon request

Created with Unity and written in C#. Made for iOS and Android.

RAM’s Scavenger Hunt will take players on a journey to uncover the Royal Alberta Museumâ€™s lost secrets! Solve riddles and track down cryptic QR codes to reveal powerful knowledge of the different exhibits.

As a programmer on this project, I was mainly resposible for front end development and implementing game features. Namely, I built QR code scanner using the ZXing.NET library, built a system to send in-app feedback to an external Google Form, and built a modular text scaler and animation scaler with Scriptable Objects. I also created a scrollable, zoomable map with touch controls and a dynamically unpdated progress checklist.

Through this process, I worked closely with other programmers, artists, and business analysts in a team of 6. I took on a leadership role and helped coordinate the team, act as a point of communication for the business analysts and members of other projectt groups, as well as orient our goals to keep the project within scope. I also co-wrote the initial Game Design Document, and made documentations for many game features to be used by the client.
